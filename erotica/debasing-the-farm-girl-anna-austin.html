“And what is your name, my dear.”

“G-Gloria,” she stuttered nervously.

I frowned and addressed her sternly. “Gloria, you will address me as 'Milord,' do you understand? If you omit my title again I shall have to put you over my knee and spank you.”

She blushed a deep red, her eyes fixed on the ground. “Yes, Milord.”

I nodded. “Good. Tell me a little of your life, Gloria.”

She looked surprised at such a request, but a glance at my face showed that I was in earnest.

“T-there is very little to tell, Milord. I was born here on your estate, and I have lived my whole life here. My father is a good man, although he sometime drinks a little too much. My mother died a year ago of the smallpox. I have no brother or sisters, though I wish I did. I have a little kitten who I have named Tabby.”

In truth, her life story was rather tedious. I doubt she had many stories to tell. Perhaps it was time to expand her experience of the world.
