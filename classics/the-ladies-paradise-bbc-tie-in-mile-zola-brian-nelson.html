The Ladies Paradise is a compelling story of ambition and love set against the backdrop of the spectacular rise of the department store in 1860s Paris. Octave Mouret is a business genius who transforms a modest drapers shop into a hugely successful retail enterprise, masterfully exploiting the desires of his female customers and ruining small competitors along the way. Through the eyes of trainee salesgirl Denise we see the inner workings of the storeand the relations and intrigues among the staff, human dramas played out alongside the relentless pursuit of commercial supremacy.Now adapted for BBC Television and given a British setting in The Paradise, Zolas novel is a masterly portrayal of life in the bustling, gossipy world of the best department store in town.
