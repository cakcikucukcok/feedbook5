For fans of the Foundation Trilogy, and Dune comes a new epic adventure in space.

The Avanauri people on the planet Prosperine are like no others in the galaxy. Prosperine didn't have an extinction event like Earth, so the most aggressive species survived...and evolved. But in 2179 of the modern era, the Avanauri look human—more or less—although they do hatch from eggs. So, it comes as a shock when one of the locals is reported to be performing miracles. The Alien Corps decide to check it out.

Prosperine’s sun is on the way to becoming a red giant, and the planet is radiation paradise. Every living thing on the ground, under the sea, and in the air, is dangerous to humans, doubly so when there’s an embargo on bringing modern weapons to the surface.

The planet does have one redeeming feature—it’s rich in the rare element crynidium, essential for faster than light travel. But Earth isn’t the only planet keenly interested in gaining mining rights.

When Commander Hickory Lace, space pilot Jess Parker, and the young genius Gareth Blanquette arrive on Prosperine to check out the situation, they become involved in a bloody revolution. Somehow, they must find a way to neutralize the alien military forces who are aiding the rebels and at the same time determine who or what the mysterious miracle worker represents.

If you enjoy epic science fiction with a touch of the supernatural and a strong female lead, then you'll love this captivating story by PJ McDermott.

Avanaux is book one in the Prosperine trilogy. Buy it today and see why readers are calling McDermott a ‘contemporary Asimov.’
